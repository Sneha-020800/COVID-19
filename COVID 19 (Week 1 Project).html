Global COVID-19 Data Analysis¶

This project analyzes the COVID-19 dataset across different countries and regions of the world.
The main objectives are:

  • To clean and prepare the dataset for analysis.
  • To identify countries/regions with the highest and lowest number of cases.
  • To explore patterns and trends using tables and visualizations.

The dataset has been taken from Kaggle in the form of Excel and CSV files.

Detailed Insights

1. South Korea

Cases started rising rapidly in mid-February 2020, mainly due to the Daegu outbreak (linked to a religious cluster).

By early March, the curve started to flatten as testing, tracing, and isolation measures were aggressively applied.

The total confirmed cases stabilized around 8,162 by March-end, showing that early and strict containment policies worked effectively.

2. Italy

Cases remained low until mid-February, then surged sharply in late February–March 2020.

4. Hubei (Wuhan, China)

The initial epicenter of the global pandemic.

Cases skyrocketed in January–February 2020, crossing 65,000 confirmed by March.

The curve plateaued quickly by March, showing that China’s strict lockdown in Wuhan was effective in halting further spread.

Unlike Italy, Hubei managed to flatten the curve within 2 months of the outbreak.

Summary¶

1. India (State-Level Analysis)

Maharashtra and Kerala emerged as the worst-hit states, followed by Delhi, Gujarat, Tamil Nadu, and Telangana.

Northern and Western India showed denser clusters of active cases compared to Northeastern and smaller Union Territories.

Urban centers (Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, Chennai, Hyderabad) were the main epicenters, highlighting the role of high population density in virus spread.

While recoveries were reported, active cases continued to dominate, showing containment challenges.

2. Global Analysis

China dominated confirmed, active, and death counts in the dataset, reflecting its role as the early epicenter of the pandemic.

Countries like Italy and South Korea showed contrasting outcomes: Italy struggled with exponential growth, while South Korea flattened its curve through aggressive testing and tracing.

India, during this period, was still in the early phase with relatively low confirmed cases and deaths compared to global hotspots.

Many African and Latin American countries reported very low cases/deaths, possibly due to actual containment or underreporting.

South Korea: Rapid spike but early flattening due to strict interventions.
